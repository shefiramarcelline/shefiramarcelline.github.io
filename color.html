In typography, color is a term with two meanings.

First, typographers will sometimes speak of a font as creating a certain color on the page—even when it’s black.
Used this way, the word encapsulates a set of hard-to-quantify characteristics like darkness, contrast, rhythm, and texture.

The second meaning is the usual one—

color as the opposite of black & white.
This was once an irrelevant topic, as most of us had to be satisfied with monochrome laser printers.
These days, color printers are ubiquitous and more writing is delivered on screen.
So color has become a practical consideration.
